Hypothesis Testing

Quantitative Variables Hypothesis Testing

Logic behind the Quantitative Variables Hypothesis Testing
Let \(X_1, X_2\) denote paired quantitative variable before and after Covid19. E.g.\(X_1\): Willingness for further study before Covid19; \(X_2\): Willingness for further study after Covid19. Let \(\mu_i,\sigma^2_i\) denote the mean and variance of variable \(X_i,(i=1,2)\). From the survey data, we know that \(X_1, X_2\) are not normally distributed.

By Central Limit Theorem. The sampling distribution of the mean for a random variable will approximate a Normal distribution when sample size is greater than 30. The sample size \(n = 80\) is actually greater than \(30\). Therefore \(\bar{X_i}\sim N(\mu_i,\frac{\sigma^2_i}{n})\). The sampling distribution of the mean of all quantitative dependent variables are approximately normal.

We also know that all quantitative dependent variables are in interval \([0,100]\). Each observation is independent of others. Therefore,we can use Paired Two Sample T Test. Since we do not know the exact direction for each test, we use two-tailed hypothesis.

Hypothesis: \(H_0\): The sampling distribution of \(\bar{X_1}\) is the same with the sampling distribution of \(\bar{X_2}\). \(H_1\): NOT the same sampling distribution.
Test Statistic: Compute by the following code.
p-value : Only the p-value of Expected stabilization of work \(0.02\) is smaller than \(\alpha=0.05\).
Conclusion: Expected stabilization of work has change significantly after Covid19 under \(\alpha=0.05\), i.e. has significant difference. Detailed interpretation about the distribution shift is needed.
Limitation: Some of the article say that Paired t-test can be used only when the difference d is normally distributed. Otherwise, it can only be checked using Shapiro-Wilk test.This is one of the ways that we can test it.

Categorical Variables Hypothesis Testing

Expected Salary

Expected salary is ordinal categorical variable. Assume the sampling distribution of expected salary before Covid19 is the expected or normal distribution. We want to know whether the distribution of expected salary after Covid19 follows the same model. In other words, we want to know whether occurrence of Covid19 is independent from expected salary level. We know that the frequency of each level is bigger than 5. Therefore, We can use chi-squared Goodness of Fit Test. Chi-Squared Test is always a two-tailed test.

Hypothesis: \(H_0\): The expected salary after Covid19 follows the distribution of expected salary before Covid19. Or occurrence of Covid19 is independent from expected salary level. \(H_1\): NOT follow hypothetical distribution, or dependent.
Test Statistic: Compute by the following code.
p-value: Way less than 0.05.
Conclusion: Occurrence of Covid19 is not independent from expected salary level under \(\alpha=0.05\). In other words, Covid19 has change the expected salary level of university students.
Limitation: the accuracy of chi-squared test.

Linear Regression

We find that the quantitative dependent variable expected stabilization of work has changed significantly after Covid19. To find out how each different independent variable will affect the expected stabilization of work, we will conduct linear regression to find the relationship.

Dependent Variable / Response Variable / Y
We use the difference of expected stabilization of work before and after Covid19 as response variable(dependent variable or y), and all the independent variables as explanatory variables(terms or x).

Explanatory Variable / Term / Predictor / X
Covid19 is the explanatory variable. Therefore, we can construct terms including /, , . However, there might exist multicollinearity. We need to distinguish active terms and inactive terms.

The distribution of (/+1) vs (abs()+1) is positive and highly right-skewed. For scale matching, we use log transformation to transform the data.

Moderator
The other variables that is not related with Covid19 is the moderators. We can use the interaction term to see the moderate effect.
